Quick Facts!!

*Blood donation is a vital act that saves lives by providing blood to patients undergoing surgeries, cancer treatments, and trauma care.
*There are four main blood types: A, B, AB, and O, each with positive (+) or negative (-) variants. Understanding blood type compatibility is essential for successful transfusions.
*Regular blood donations are crucial for maintaining an adequate blood supply, as blood has a limited shelf life. Donating regularly ensures blood banks can meet patient needs.
*Donors must meet specific criteria based on age, weight, health, and recent travel history. Certain medical conditions or recent surgeries may temporarily disqualify individuals from donating.
*Platelets, essential for clotting, are often needed by patients with cancer, organ transplants, and other conditions. Platelet donors can donate more frequently than whole blood donors.
*Blood donors may experience health benefits such as reduced disease risk and improved cardiovascular health. Additionally, donating blood can provide a sense of fulfillment and purpose.
*Blood donation relies on the generosity of donors, volunteers, and blood donation centers. By donating blood, individuals contribute to community well-being and make a positive societal impact.
